Week 6

For our last week, we focused on completing the arch and the sliding guide for the rail system done.

Moving beyond a simple sheet of styrene to hold down the food, we looked for more complex arches that offered still offered flexibility. After extensive searching, we found that a joinable cable chain was a good fit: each piece could be added to increase the length, and the chain remained flexible. We then modified the 3D file (image 2) so that it was easier to push at the top compared to the original design.

After 3D printing these pieces, we found that their range of motion was too limited for our use case. We redesigned the pieces and filed the appropriate parts to improve flexibility. For the pieces near the rail, we filed the front of the ball joint which allowing them to bend forward at a greater angle so the chain link could sit closer to the board with fewer pieces.

For the center pieces, we filed the back of the ball joint to allow backward bending, which was necessary for the section users would press to hold the food down.

Additionally, for the rail system, we created another guide to help the user stabilize the knife and slide it smoothly along the rail.
