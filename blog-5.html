Week 5

This week was focused on getting the actual board done

After extensive consultation in the Solid Space Lab (shoutout to Maja), we realized that our original proposal with the fixed angle holders was not feasible. Inspired by the guide track on the table saw and our mediocre results with moving the board to reposition the food, we decided to replace the orginal holder placements with a beveled rail system, which both allowed the holders to move along the board and was simplified enough to make with the existing tools in the Solid Space lab.

Below are our orthographic views and 3d model of the modified board

Creating the board

To create the board, we started with two-by-four wood. First, we angled the table saw to 30 degrees and cut the wood along its width into strips with both angled and straight edges.

Next, we used a chop saw to cut the strips to the appropriate lengths, and finally, we glued the pieces together with wood glue to assemble the finished board.
