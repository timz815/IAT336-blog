Assistive Cutting Board

An innovative approach to making food preparation more accessible for individuals with limited mobility or dexterity.

Week 4

Week 3

This week, after multiple rounds of refinement, we progressed to creating a full-scale prototype as we began to solidify and work out the final direction and details of our design.

Week 2

After deciding to move forward with the assistive cutting board concept, we transitioned from ideation to hands on exploration through low fidelity prototyping. We used styrene, foam, cardstock, and cardboard to build multiple phsyical models to better understand structural integriy, ergonomics, and dimensional relationships.
